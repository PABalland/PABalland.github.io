The East African Paradox: Kenya ($2,900 GDP/capita) and Ethiopia ($1,020 GDP/capita) produce runners with average top times 18+ minutes faster than the USA ($80,000 GDP/capita). Running ability defies conventional development theory — motivation to escape poverty is itself a performance driver, documented in peer-reviewed sports science literature.

Why East Africa dominates

86% of elite Kenyan runners ran to school daily as children vs 23% of non-athletes. Running at altitude raises VO₂max by up to 30%. Prize money at majors can reach $100,000+ — life-changing in Kenya.
