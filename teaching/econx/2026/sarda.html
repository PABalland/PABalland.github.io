High severity
Platform commission drain
15–30%
Every order through UberEats or DoorDash costs 15–30% off the top. At 30% on a $25 order, $7.50 is gone before food, labor, or rent. Deliveroo Editions can reach 40%.

Source: OpenTable · ScienceDirect 2025

High severity
Year-one failure rate
~60%
Up to 60% of ghost kitchens fail in their first year — driven by poor branding, inadequate market research, and underestimated ramp-up costs. Only ~40% survive year one.

Source: Restroworks

High severity
Algorithm dependency
Instant impact
A single platform ranking change can halve order volume overnight. With no physical storefront to fall back on, dark kitchens are entirely exposed to third-party rule changes.

Source: Plastic Container City

Medium severity
Brand invisibility
No storefront
Without a physical presence, building customer loyalty is an uphill battle. Operators depend entirely on platform reviews, social media, and digital marketing to remain visible.

Source: OpenTable

Medium severity
8–10 week cash-burn period
€8,000+ needed
Dark kitchens typically lose money for 8–10 weeks while building platform visibility and review scores. At least €8,000 in working capital is recommended before launch.

Source: KitchenNmbrs

Medium severity
Food quality in transit
$0.50–$1.50/order
Packaging costs add up, and quality degrades during delivery. Poor packaging damages ratings, which then hurts platform ranking — a compounding loop that's hard to escape.

Source: KitchenNmbrs

Lower severity
Regulatory ambiguity
Varies by city
Many cities lack clear rules for delivery-only operators. Permits, zoning, and food safety inspections are required but inconsistently enforced across online aggregators.

Source: ScienceDirect 2025

Lower severity
Market saturation
Metro areas
Major cities see growing competition from established restaurants pivoting to delivery, virtual brands from large chains, and a steady wave of new ghost kitchen entrants.

Source: Plastic Container City
