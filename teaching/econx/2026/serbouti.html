How this simulator works

Every figure has a source. Every source has a labelled orientation. Here is exactly where each number comes from.

01
Official programmes & declarations
Each policy analysed comes from candidates' public declarations, official programmes, or documented positions in verified national media (franceinfo, Le Monde, Le Figaro, Public Sénat).
02
Institutional data first
INSEE, Banque de France, OECD, INED, CAE: official sources with no party affiliation. Our primary reference for all baseline figures.
03
Sources labelled by orientation
Institut Montaigne (centre-liberal), IFRAP (right-liberal), OFCE/Sciences Po (academic), IPP (academic). We state their leanings so you can assess their biases.
04
Ranges, not certainties
All projections are ranges. Macroeconomic modelling involves genuine uncertainty — especially for programmes that have never been implemented. All figures are extrapolated from 2022 analyses updated with 2026 baseline data.

About this project

Who built this — and why

France 2027 Policy Simulator is an independent civic education project. No political party funded it. No candidate is recommended.

🔍
Full transparency
Every figure has a source. Every source has a labelled political orientation. You can verify everything yourself — every claim links to its original document.
⚖️
Non-partisan
No candidate is recommended. No political option is presented as superior. We present what the research says — including where research is uncertain or contested.
🎓
Educational purpose only
This is not a prediction tool. Economic projections are estimates based on 2022 analyses. Real policy outcomes depend on parliamentary balance, markets, and implementation.
✉️
Found an error?
If you find an incorrect figure or missing source, we want to know. This project is correctable and will be updated as official 2027 programmes are published. Contact: [email protected]
What this site is NOT:
