The Problem

Aid is one of humanity's most powerful levers for good. Yet chronic data illiteracy makes it an easy political target when budgets tighten. With USAID dismantled and aid budgets under fire globally, the field needs a compelling, data-driven counter-narrative — urgently. These aren't edge cases. They're symptoms of a systemic failure to connect the dots.

// DATA FRAGMENTATION Thousands of data sources — OECD, IATI, bilateral donors, NGOs — exist in incompatible siloes with no unified intelligence layer.
// BURIED KNOWLEDGE Critical insights sit in unread PDFs: inputs, outputs, lessons learned — inaccessible to the very people making next decisions.
// INVISIBLE IMPACT Aid's effectiveness is invisible to publics and parliaments, making it a perennial scapegoat when budgets are cut.
// RECIPIENT EXCLUSION Recipient countries lack the data to negotiate, plan, or hold donors to commitments — aid flows are opaque to those they serve.
The Solution

A live, open observatory that connects, analyzes, and visualizes the world's aid data — giving every stakeholder the intelligence they need to act.

Who Uses It & For What
Recipient Governments Know what is coming, what is promised, and where gaps are — negotiate from strength and plan national development strategies with full visibility.
Donor Agencies Identify duplication, spot underfunded sectors, and demonstrate impact to skeptical parliaments and publics with real-time evidence.
Civil Society Hold donors accountable — call out ODA funds quietly redirected to domestic refugee costs instead of promised development work.
Multilaterals & Research Surface systemic patterns — which water projects in Sub-Saharan Africa are at risk? Where should the next $10M in health funding actually go?

THE MOMENT IS NOW

With USAID dismantled and aid budgets under fire globally, the field needs a compelling, data-driven counter-narrative — urgently. The Observatory is that counter-narrative. We begin with OECD data to expose ODA misdirection, build a pipeline to unlock knowledge trapped in PDFs, and expand into a live platform connecting needs with resources worldwide. The data infrastructure exists. The tools exist. What's missing is the will to connect them — and a home to make it happen.
